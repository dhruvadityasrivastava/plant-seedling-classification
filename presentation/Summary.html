The Challenge

Accurately identifying plant seedlings is a critical challenge in modern agriculture. Manual differentiation between crops and weeds is slow, labor-intensive, and prone to error. This inefficiency leads to incorrect herbicide application, reduced crop yield, and significant financial losses. The goal is to automate this process for faster, more reliable results.

Our 4-Step Solution

1

Data Analysis

Analyzed the distribution of 12 seedling species.

2

Preprocessing

Resized, normalized, and augmented images.

4

Evaluation

Compared models to find the top performer.

Understanding the Data

The project utilized a dataset of 4,750 images across 12 species. Exploratory Data Analysis revealed a key challenge: a notable class imbalance, where some species had significantly more samples than others.

Model Performance: A Head-to-Head Battle

Three models were developed. The comparison clearly shows the superiority of transfer learning (Model 3) and highlights the severe overfitting and poor performance of the custom-built CNNs.

The Winner: VGG16 Transfer Learning

Model 3, which leverages the pre-trained VGG16 architecture, was the undisputed champion. By using weights trained on a massive dataset (ImageNet), the model could generalize far more effectively than networks trained from scratch. This approach significantly reduced training time and produced a robust model ready for real-world application, achieving an impressive 87.5% validation accuracy.

Recommendations

  • Deploy Model 3

    The VGG16 model is robust and ready for implementation in agricultural technology applications.

  • Address Data Imbalance

    Enhance accuracy by gathering more images for under-represented species like 'Common wheat' and 'Maize'.

  • Fine-Tune Hyperparameters

    Further performance gains can be achieved by tuning the learning rate and number of trainable layers.
